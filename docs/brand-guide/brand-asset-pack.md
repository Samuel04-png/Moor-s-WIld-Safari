# Brand Asset Pack

## Brand Position

Moor's Wild Safaris should feel warm, premium, grounded, trustworthy, and personal. The logo suggests sunset light, acacia silhouettes, elephants, a safari vehicle, canvas camp, and overland exploration. The digital brand translates those signals into spacious editorial layouts, tactile color, restrained motion, and practical planning language.

## Logo Usage

- Primary logo: `assets/logos/moors-wild-safaris-logo-transparent.png`
- Full logo with white background: `assets/logos/moors-wild-safaris-logo-full.jpeg`
- Clear space: keep at least the height of the word "Moor's" around the mark.
- Minimum digital width: 96px for compact header use, 160px or larger for footer and brand boards.
- Do not stretch, recolor, blur, place on low-contrast busy backgrounds, or crop off the vehicle, tent, or tagline.

## Typography

- Headlines: Georgia stack for editorial warmth and classic safari-lodge character.
- Body and UI: system sans stack for readability, speed, and maintainability.
- Letter spacing: normal, to avoid brittle small text.
- Headline style: short, confident, human, not over-written.

## UI System

- Radius: 8px maximum for cards and image panels.
- Buttons: rounded pills for primary actions, with strong contrast.
- Cards: used only for repeated items, testimonials, and framed content.
- Sections: full-width bands or unframed layouts with constrained inner width.
- Motion: gentle reveal and image scale on hover, disabled under reduced motion.
- Forms: visible labels, required validation, clear focus states.

## Photography Direction

Use real client photography when available. Prioritize:

- Golden-hour wildlife and vehicle moments.
- Mobile camp detail and canvas texture.
- Lodge decks, river light, and quiet comfort.
- Guide or vehicle details over staged faces.
- Cultural travel moments handled respectfully and contextually.
- Clear landscapes that show Zambia and Southern Africa, not anonymous luxury.

Avoid:

- Over-processed sunsets.
- Unsafe wildlife proximity.
- Generic stock smiles.
- Stereotyped props or costume imagery.
- Dark, cropped images where the product is hard to inspect.

## Tone of Voice

Use calm, confident language. Favor specific planning reassurance over big claims.

Good:
- "The route is shaped around season, pace, and comfort."
- "Movement is planned with the same care as the safari."
- "Start with the journey you have in mind."

Avoid:
- "Once in a lifetime adventure awaits."
- "Book now before it is too late."
- "Unforgettable memories in paradise."
