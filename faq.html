Planning answers

Safari questions, answered plainly.

Clear answers help guests feel supported before the first proposal is written.

How far ahead should we plan?

For peak dry-season safaris and smaller lodges, earlier planning gives better choice. Flexible travelers can still build strong routes closer to departure.

Can one trip combine lodges and mobile camping?

Yes. A mixed route can balance comfort, immersion, and logistics when the sequence is planned carefully.

Do you arrange transfers?

Yes. Airport, inter-city, cross-border, private, and corporate transfers can be arranged as standalone services or part of a safari.

Are photographic safaris different?

They can be. Photography-led trips consider vehicle setup, light, patience at sightings, and destinations that match the subject priorities.

Can families or private groups travel?

Yes. The itinerary should account for ages, pace, comfort, rooming, drive times, and how much structure the group wants.

Can you plan cross-border routes?

Yes. Regional extensions can include Botswana, Zimbabwe, Namibia, Malawi, or South Africa with transfer and border details handled in the plan.
